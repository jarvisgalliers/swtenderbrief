3. Where your card details go

When you pay, you enter your card details directly into Stripe’s payment form. We never see or store your full card number. Stripe is the payment processor; it acts as our processor for handling the payment and as a controller in its own right for fraud-prevention and its regulatory obligations. Stripe’s privacy policy: https://stripe.com/privacy.

4. Who else we share it with (our processors)

We keep the list short on purpose:

  • Stripe — payment processing (paid tier only). Where Stripe processes data outside the UK, it does so under the UK’s international transfer safeguards.
  • Resend — sends the digest emails on our behalf and provides delivery/open/click and unsubscribe handling. It processes your email address and engagement data as our processor under a data-processing agreement, including via infrastructure outside the UK under appropriate safeguards.
  • GitHub (GitHub Pages) — hosts this website; sees standard server-log information (IP address, timestamp) for requests to the site, kept briefly for security. We don’t run advertising or third-party analytics trackers on the site.

We don’t sell your data, don’t share it for anyone else’s marketing, and don’t use it to build profiles for advertising. We may disclose data if we’re legally required to (e.g. a valid request from HMRC or a court).

5. How long we keep it

  • While you’re subscribed: for as long as you have a free or paid subscription.
  • After you unsubscribe / cancel: we remove your email and filter preferences from the active mailing list promptly, and in any event within 30 days, unless you’ve asked us to keep you on the free tier.
  • Payment and tax records: kept for 6 years after the end of the relevant tax year, because HMRC requires it. Kept minimal (amounts, dates, Stripe IDs) — not your card details.
  • Support emails: kept for up to 2 years then deleted.
  • Suppression list: if you unsubscribe, we keep a minimal record (a hash of your email) so we don’t accidentally email you again — you can ask us to delete even that.

6. Cookies / website

The South-West Tender Brief website is a simple static site. It does not set advertising or cross-site tracking cookies. The Stripe checkout pages set their own cookies, governed by Stripe’s policy. If we ever add analytics, we’ll update this notice and ask for consent where required.

7. Your rights

Under the UK GDPR you have the right to: access the personal data we hold about you; have inaccurate data corrected; have your data erased where it applies (for the digest, that’s as simple as unsubscribing, but you can also ask us to wipe everything including the suppression hash); restrict or object to processing in certain circumstances (including objecting to the legitimate-interests engagement-analytics use); data portability; and to withdraw consent where we rely on it.

To exercise any of these, email privacy@swtenderbrief.co.uk. We’ll respond within one month. We won’t charge you (unless a request is excessive or repetitive). We may need to confirm your identity (e.g. that you control the email address).

8. Complaints

If you’re unhappy with how we’ve handled your data, please tell us first — privacy@swtenderbrief.co.uk. You also have the right to complain to the UK regulator:

Information Commissioner’s Office — https://ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint/ — helpline 0303 123 1113 — Wycliffe House, Water Lane, Wilmslow, Cheshire SK9 5AF.

9. Changes to this notice

If we change this notice in a way that matters to you, we’ll tell you by email and/or in a digest. The “last updated” date at the top always shows the current version.
