Is this for me?

You’re a good fit if you’re a sole trader or small firm in South-West England doing any of: building & general construction · electrical · plumbing & heating · drainage · roofing · carpentry & joinery · plastering · tiling · painting & decorating · flooring · kitchen/bathroom fitting · groundworks & paving · fencing · grounds maintenance · tree surgery · commercial cleaning · locksmith/security · signage · fire alarm/CCTV.

You’re probably not a fit if you only want jobs over £100k, or you’re outside the South West (we cover roughly BS, BA, TA, EX, TQ, PL, TR, DT, and the SW parts of GL, SP, SN, BH — Bristol down to Cornwall, across to Dorset).

How it works

  1. You tell us your trades and your postcode areas. (Free tier: skip this — you get everything, region-wide.)
  2. We do the trawling. Every day we pull the new opportunities from the official sources — Contracts Finder, Find a Tender, and South-West council procurement portals — work out which ones a one-or-two-person outfit could actually win, and tag them by trade.
  3. You get an email. Free: once a month, everything in the South West. Paid: every week, only the ones matching your trades and areas, sent ahead of the monthly round-up, with a reminder before each closing date. Each listing is one line — buyer, title, rough value, closing date, a “why you’re seeing this”, and a link straight to the official notice. Read it in two minutes; chase the ones you fancy.

What it is not (managing expectations, on purpose)

  • It’s not a tender portal — you still submit your bid on the council’s own system; we just point you at the opportunity.
  • It’s not a guarantee — we pull from public sources and use AI to sort and summarise; we can miss things, and the official notice always wins over our summary. Always click through and read the real notice before you rely on anything.
  • It won’t write your bid — it tells you what’s out there; the quote’s still down to you.
  • It won’t cold-call anyone or sell your details — your email is used to send you the brief, full stop. (Privacy notice.)

FAQ

Where do the opportunities come from?

Official, public sources: GOV.UK’s Contracts Finder, the Find a Tender Service, and South-West local-authority procurement portals (e.g. the Supplying the South West portal that Devon, Cornwall, Plymouth, Somerset and others use). Public-sector procurement data — used under the Open Government Licence v3.0.

How do you decide what’s “worth a look”?

A scoring rubric: is it in the South West, is the value in sole-trader range (we focus under £50k, surface a bit above with a note), does the work match a trade, is there enough time to quote, is the submission process manageable (not a 40-page PQQ). Anything borderline is flagged rather than dropped. It’s not perfect — that’s what the “see the real notice” link is for.

Will I get spammed?

No. Free tier: one email a month. Paid: one email a week, plus the odd deadline reminder. One-click unsubscribe on every email. We don’t sell, rent or share your address, and we don’t send marketing for anything else.

Can I change my trades or postcode areas?

Yes — link in every weekly email. Add or drop trades, widen or narrow your patch, any time.

What if I cancel?

Cancel any time from the link in your emails. You keep the weekly brief until the end of the year you’ve paid for, then drop to the free monthly one (or off the list entirely, your call). New subscribers also get a 14-day full-refund window. (Refund policy.)

Do you cover Scotland / Wales / the rest of England?

Not yet — this one’s South-West England only, on purpose. Doing one region properly beats doing the whole UK badly.

Is my data safe / GDPR and all that?

We hold your email and (paid) your trade/area preferences and a Stripe payment record — nothing more. We’re registered with the ICO, Stripe handles the card details (we never see them), and you’ve got the usual rights to see/correct/delete your data. Full details in the Privacy notice.
